Aspects of the creation of Tier 2

Pension plans in 2008 were going to court to reduce the cost-of-living increases. We did not think that would be, or, honestly, should be, successful.Staff's view was that a cost-of-living adjustment is an important part of a promised, contractual, benefit.

To avoid legal issues, and to bring down pension liabilities, the cost-of-living adjustment on the Tier 2 Regular Benefit became discretionary, unlike the Regular Benefit for Tier 1 participants.

This change provides a way for a future board to reduce liabilies by having a policy to not increase the benefit. Members of this Board has expressed some concern that the increase is not tied to an outside measure of interest rates. I think that the function of it being discretionary must not be compromised. The Board must contemplate this difficult decision every year.
